My Happiness Is...

Eating 'til It No Longer Hurts

CAUTION: CHILDHOOD STORY

There are no foods I won't try. More than that, there are no foods I won't keep trying. My philosophy is: I don't like it, yet.

As a child I was forced to eat foods I really disliked. I remember this one Chinese broth that tasted like feet. My siblings and I sat at the table with these small bowls of feet soup and as I stared at my soup, steaming stink into the air I couldn't understand how anyone would enjoy this. I watched as my brother gulped it down as quickly as possible. Then I turned to my right and my sister was using a straw so she couldn't taste it. I couldn't bring myself to drink it so I just sat there. My mother would always say that I would appreciate it when I got older because I would enjoy all foods. She was right.

I have learned that if I don't like the way something tastes, or its texture, or aroma - all I have to do is keep eating it and eventually I'll start to crave it!

And It Was All Yellow

Food doesn't have to be yellow but it could be...

Color is perception. Humans have an innate appreciation for color as well as a culturally adapted one.

CAUTION: ANOTHER CHILDHOOD STORY

When I was about seven the Might Morphin Power Rangers were a huge hit. My brother and I used to go over to a friend's house to watch it because they had a television. I identified with the Yellow Ranger, probably because she was Asian and the Pink Ranger's girlie persona irritated me. I don't know if this is why yellow is my favorite color, but it is the first time I remember being absolutely infatuated and addicted to yellow.

99 Designs notes an almost instantaneous reaction to colors and also seems to coincidentally find statistics with the number 9:
People decide whether or not they like a product in 90 seconds or less. 90% of that decision is based solely on color.
